From Tennis Courts to Pickleball: Facility Conversion in Queensland

A snapshot of how Queensland’s current pickleball supply is being delivered — and where venue formats are trending.

Samuel Potter Wednesday February 4, 2026 Sport Facilities
Pickleball Venues in Queensland by Facility Type (%)
Venue formats QLD Market structure

Pickleball venues in Queensland continue to be delivered across a mix of facility types, with tennis facilities (27%) and gyms (26%) accounting for more than half of current supply. This reflects the sport’s early growth phase, where existing infrastructure has played a key role in meeting demand.

Looking ahead, we anticipate a continued rise in dedicated pickleball venues, supported by strong year-on-year participation growth. As this trend accelerates, the overall share of repurposed facilities is expected to decline, with operators increasingly prioritising purpose-built venues to support programming, capacity and long-term scalability.

Feasibility note: Facility type influences operating outcomes — repurposed sites may lower entry cost, while dedicated formats can unlock higher utilisation through programming control, peak-time capacity, and scalable court layouts.
